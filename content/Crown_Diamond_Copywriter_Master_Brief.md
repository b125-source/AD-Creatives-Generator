# **Part 4 — Voice, Tone & Language Guide**

Crown Diamond must sound different from every competitor in the market. Here is the exact voice and tone to write in — by language and by audience segment.

## **4.1 The Crown Diamond Brand Voice**

| Dimension | Direction |
| :---- | :---- |
| Overall Feel | Warm authority. Expert but approachable. Like a trusted gemstone dealer who also happens to be a friend. |
| NOT Like... | A corporate brochure (GLAZE). An aggressive discount warehouse (Petiwala). An anonymous catalogue (Fair Deal). |
| Confidence Level | Quietly confident. Never boastful. Let the stone and the story do the work. |
| Human vs. Corporate | Always human. Mr. Tarachand is the brand. The name 'Crown Diamond' carries regality — let the copy carry warmth. |
| Length | Short and punchy for hooks. Medium for body copy. Never long. Luxury buyers do not read essays. |
| Specificity | Always specific over vague. 'Italian Calacatta from Carrara' not 'imported marble.' '200+ varieties' not 'huge collection.' |
| Emoji Use | Sparingly. 1–2 per post maximum. Use: ✦ 🏛️ 💎 ✨ for premium feel. Avoid: 🔥 💥 ‼️ (cheap discount feel). |

## **4.2 English Voice — B2C HNI (Villa Owners, Premium Buyers)**

* Register: Elevated lifestyle. Think: Architectural Digest, not Lulu Hypermarket.

* Sentence length: Short. Punchy. Let sentences breathe.

* Key words TO USE: signature, curated, sourced, crafted, exclusive, one-of-a-kind, direct, natural, timeless, elevate, transform.

* Key words TO AVOID: cheap, deal, discount (unless offer ad), best price, affordable, quality (overused — SHOW don't tell quality).

| ENGLISH TONE EXAMPLE — B2C | "Your floor is the first thing people feel when they walk in. Make it say something. Crown Diamond brings Italy's finest marble to your Dubai doorstep — direct from source, at prices the middlemen don't want you to know about." — This is the register. Confident. Slightly conspiratorial ('prices they don't want you to know'). Specific. Aspirational. |
| :---: | :---- |

## **4.3 English Voice — B2B (Interior Designers, Architects, Contractors)**

* Register: Peer-to-peer professional. You understand their world.

* Lead with their problem, not your product: 'Sourcing reliable marble for a project deadline?' not 'We sell marble.'

* Key words TO USE: reliable supply, consistent quality lot-to-lot, custom fabrication, technical data available, trade pricing, project delivery.

* Key words TO AVOID: luxury, aspirational, lifestyle (these feel irrelevant to a contractor reading the ad at 7am on a construction site).

| ENGLISH TONE EXAMPLE — B2B | "Sourcing stone for a project? Crown Diamond is Dubai's direct importer — no middlemen, no markups, no delays. 200+ varieties. Custom cuts to spec. WhatsApp us your requirement and get pricing within the hour." — Professional. Efficient. Answers their need before they ask. |
| :---: | :---- |

## **4.4 Hindi / Hinglish Voice — Indian HNI & NRI Community**

This is Crown Diamond's secret weapon. No competitor is using this. The tone must feel like a trusted family business speaking to their own community — not a corporate brand translating English into Hindi.

* Register: Warm, slightly desi-business-uncle energy. Like someone you trust at a family gathering recommending something genuinely good.

* Mix: 60% Hindi / 40% English is the sweet spot for educated Indian HNIs in Dubai. Don't go 100% Hindi (feels low-quality). Don't go 100% English (misses the point).

* Script choice: Use Devanagari (Hindi script) for primary text. Roman transliteration for supporting copy. Both appear in same creative.

* Key phrases TO USE: apka ghar (your home), sabse behtareen (the very best), seedha import (direct import), koi middleman nahi (no middleman), apne sapno ka ghar (your dream home), quality mein koi compromise nahi (no compromise on quality).

| HINGLISH TONE EXAMPLE | "Dubai ke sabse acche marble, seedha Italy aur Turkey se. Koi middleman nahi — seedha aapke paas. Apne villa ke liye best chahiye? Crown Diamond pe ek WhatsApp karo." (English translation: 'The finest marble in Dubai, direct from Italy and Turkey. No middleman — straight to you. Want the best for your villa? Send one WhatsApp to Crown Diamond.') — Warm. Direct. Community tone. |
| :---: | :---- |

## **4.5 Arabic Voice — Emirati & Arab HNI**

Arabic creative should be written by a native Arabic speaker or reviewed by one. The tone is formal luxury — not casual. Emirati HNIs respond to elevated classical Arabic register for luxury goods.

* Register: Formal. Elegant. Poetic if possible. Reference the concept of 'رقي' (refinement) and 'فخامة' (luxury/grandeur).

* Use the Alfan Almeamary approach as your model: 'عالم تتجسد فيه الفخامة في كل لوح حجر' — 'A world where luxury materializes in every stone slab.'

* CTA in Arabic: 'تواصل معنا عبر واتساب' (Contact us via WhatsApp). Direct and culturally appropriate.

* NOTE TO TEAM: Flag Arabic copy for native speaker review before going live. Do not publish unreviewed Arabic.

| Mistake | Who Does It | Crown Diamond Approach |

| English only — ignoring Indian community | ALL 12 competitors | Hindi/Hinglish for every campaign. At minimum, one ad set per campaign in Hindi. |

| Stopping ads after 3 days (testing) | Petiwala — tested 10 variants then stopped all | Allow 30 days minimum before evaluating. Meta algorithm needs 50+ events to optimise. Be patient. |

| All video OR all image | Several competitors use only one format | Test both. Some audiences stop for video. Others respond to image. Run both in every campaign. |
| Ignoring Arabic HNI segment | Most English-only advertisers | Crown Diamond: English \+ Hindi \+ Arabic \= three untapped audiences in one campaign. |
| No social proof / no named projects | Almost every stone advertiser is anonymous | Name projects. Collect testimonials. 'Ahmad Naqi Villa' style specificity (5/9 Design) \= maximum credibility. |
